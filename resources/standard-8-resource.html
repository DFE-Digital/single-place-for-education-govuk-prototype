Running a retrospective
Retrospectives, sometimes called ‘retros’, are regular meetings where your whole team talks about
what’s going well and what isn’t.
The point of a retro is to fix any problems and to make sure you keep doing the things that are
working.
Time: 30 to 45 minutes
What you need: Pens, post-its, wall space
Who’s it for: Teaching staff or pupils
Difficulty: Easy
How to run a retro
Retros should have an open atmosphere where every member of the team can speak honestly and
feel confident that their colleagues will listen.
The teacher asking for feedback should host the retro.
1.
The teacher explains the questions at the beginning and sticks a post-it note to the wall for
each question.
2.
Each teacher or pupil writes down one or more answers for each question on post-it notes
and sticks them to the right part of the wall.
3.
The group discusses issues as they come up, or at the end.
4.
The host decides on actions to fix any problems raised.
Questions for the retro
Ask the members of the group to write down:
● what you’re good at
● what your weaknesses are
● what you could do that would make a difference to them (ideally used if you’re running a
retro with your students)
How to analyse the main issues
You should use the information you get from your retro to improve your work and your working
environment.
Make a list of actions that you’ll carry out to fix the problems that your colleagues or pupils have
highlighted.
