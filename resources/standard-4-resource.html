Improving the appropriate wait time between
question and response in class
Aims and objectives
A newly qualified teacher (NQT) needs to help pupils think about their answers so they can
develop a:
● wider vocabulary
● more developed response
This resource will help an NQT to:
● elicit a more developed response from their class
● learn how to gauge the wait time between question and response
This resource specifically helps NQTs to develop as required in standard 4 of the Early Career
Framework: Plan and teach well structured lessons
Time: 30 minutes
What you need: whiteboards, thesaurus’
Who it’s for: pupils
Difficulty: hard
How to run this exercise
1. Prepare a list of questions you want to ask your pupils. These can be questions on
feelings, thoughts, concepts or just describing things or events using adjectives.
Examples questions include ‘How do you feel about the environment?’
2. Introduce the exercise to your class.
3. Give each student a thesaurus and their own whiteboard.
4. Write the question you want to ask up on your whiteboard.
5. Ask each pupil for a one-word answer.
6. After each pupil has answered, tell them to find a suitable synonym using a thesaurus if
necessary.
7. Create a leaders’ chart that lists the top 3 pupils’ answers in terms of complexity.
8. Repeat the exercise until you have run through your questions.
